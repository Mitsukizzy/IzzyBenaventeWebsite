// Developer, Hackathoner, Gamer, and Foodie?


I'm a huge fan of building cool things for fun. I love messing with new technologies and I have some cool tech like an Tobii eye tracker and a Hexoskin biometric shirt that I plan on hacking on next. I often spend time exploring LA, looking for the best eats. When all else fails, I'll be on my PS4 or Steam trying to catch up on the latest games.

Hi, I'm Izzy


I'm an avid gamer, serial hackathoner, and coffee lover currently residing in Los Angeles, CA. I love hot and sunny cities, especially ones near the water. On the weekends, I used to paddle for the USC Dragon Boat team, but now I only have time for occasional games of tennis and beach volleyball. My favorite video games include Journey, Final Fantasy, Assassin's Creed, Life is Strange, and the Tomb Raider reboot.

Originally from the island of Guam, I moved to Las Vegas with my family when I was eight. I studied Computer Science throughout all four years of high school, realizing along the way that it would be a dream come true if I could develop games for a living. Fast-forward to the present and now I'm a third year undergraduate studying Computer Science with a focus in Game Development and Cyber Security at the University of Southern California.

Throughout my entire second year at USC, I worked on a virtual reality game called ElemenTerra as a Gameplay Programmer. I was responsible for programming many of the player-specific functions and also for experimenting and developing the game's flight controls using the Razer Hydra and the Oculus Rift. This year, I've been working on a narrative-driven, detective, FireTV game called Psychic as the Lead Engineer.

In the summer 2014, I was a Web Development Intern at Slickdeals, which is like the reddit for the best deals on the internet. I programmed new web pages in HTML, CSS, PHP, and SQL. This past summer (2015) I worked at Infinity Ward on the UI team as a Software Engineer Intern. It was a fantastic experience so I'm going back to IW for my summer 2016 internship.

I'm currently the Vice President of USC's ACM chapter which organizes events, both professional and social, for students with an interest in Computer Science.

Parsly - Web Developer

Parsly allows users to retrieve important email data with only 1 bar of 2G data. Our PHP server in the background constantly polls for any text messages from your phone number and if it detects a valid command such as 'amazon' or 'itinerary' it will parse through your emails and send you the details that you want see. The website also has features which allow you to easily unsubscribe to mailing lists, or access details of your future events. We accomplish these features by filtering emails by tags such as unsubscribe, eventbrite, and itinerary. I programmed the website's dashboard.

DevPost Submission

WearHacks

Los Angeles, CA
November 2015

Where's It - Android and Pebble Watch Developer

We used Estimote beacons to identify different rooms in the building and then we used Estimote stickers to tag our individual items such as a laptop or a backpack. These beacons and stickers send the list of items into our Firebase database where we keep track of which stickers are located in each room. You can view the location of each item on the android app and your watch. If you have many items with stickers, you could easily use the voice dictation feature on the watch and say "backpack" and your pebble watch will then display the name of the room where your backpack is located. I designed the pebble app, programmed it, and made it communicate with our android app. I also implemented the voice dictation feature on the pebble app.
